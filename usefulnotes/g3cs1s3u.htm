Extract from a booklet written by Labour MP Keir Hardie in September 1906 calling for women's suffrage
(PRO ref: PRO 30/69/1834)		  
  • The Labour Party was a small but growing force in 1906. In the 1906 election it gained 29 seats. Eventually it would go on to replace the Liberals as one of the two biggest parties in British politics.
  • Keir Hardie was a leading figure in the Labour movement. He was Labour's first MP in the 1890s and represented the Welsh steel town of Merthyr Tydfil from 1900-15.
  • Both Hardie and the Labour Party supported votes for women. They spent much of their time campaigning for better health, education and working conditions for ordinary people. Labour saw female suffrage as simply another example of an unfair law which needed to be changed.
  • Hardie refers to 'Half angel, half idiot'. This is a reference to the view of women as the angel who looked after the home and family. It was one reason why some men opposed women's suffrage. They thought politics was a dirty business and did not want angelic women involved. Some men saw women as irrational idiots, and that is why they opposed the vote.
  • At the end of this extract Hardie quotes from Disraeli. This was Benjamin Disraeli, Conservative Prime Minister in the 1860s and 1870s. He was the driving force behind the 1867 Reform Act which extended the vote to some working men. Hardie probably quoted a Conservative politician deliberately to make his point that female suffrage was not a party political issue but an issue of principle.
