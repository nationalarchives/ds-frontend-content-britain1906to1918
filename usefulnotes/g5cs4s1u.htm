Extract from a government report on the effects of war on the economy, September 1914
(PRO ref: CAB 37/121/106)		  
  • In 1914 the British economy was not seen as the responsibility of the government. It was very much the job of businesses to make money.
  • However, the government did monitor overall trends like prices, wages, firms closing, firms struggling, firms doing well etc.
  • As the war went on, the government used this information to see how well firms were able to supply the war materials that the country needed. By 1917 the government actually took over some key industries like coal and munitions. It also closely controlled other industries.
  • This document shows how the war affected the British economy in its very early stages. Many workers lost their jobs as contracts were cancelled. However, war work soon mean that there was plenty of work and that wages went up as well.
