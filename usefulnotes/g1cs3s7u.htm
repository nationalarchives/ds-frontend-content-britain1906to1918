Extract from a report in 1909 to the British Cabinet by Winston Churchill on National Insurance and Labour Exchanges
(PRO ref: CAB 37/99/69)		  
  • This document was presented to the Cabinet in April 1909, soon after the Liberals had introduced a national system of Labour Exchanges.
  • The author of the report was Winston Churchill. He was an enthusiastic supporter of the idea of Labour Exchanges. He was also a key figure in the Liberal reforms, along with David Lloyd George.
  • There had been Labour Exchanges before the Liberals brought in their reforms. One of the problems was that not all workers and all trades and industries used them. This meant that there were often cases when Exchanges did not know about jobs and employers who needed workers did not tell the Exchanges.
  • Churchill's planned National Insurance Act would cover all workers. This meant that the government would have had a lot more information about how many workers were unemployed and in which industries. As a result, the government Labour Exchanges would be better informed about jobs and workers.
