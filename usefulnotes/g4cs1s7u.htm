Report from the Daily Express on the coroner's inquest into the death of Emily Davison, June 1913
(PRO ref: HO 144/1150/210.696)		  
  • Emily Wilding Davison was a militant Suffragette who was well known for her daring and dangerous exploits in trying to promote the cause of women's suffrage. She had once hidden herself inside the Houses of Parliament. She had also set fire to letter-boxes.
  • She was arrested many times and had often been to prison. While in prison she had thrown herself down stairs as a protest.
  • She was somewhat on the fringe of the Suffragette movement. Even the militant Pankhursts saw her as an unstable character.
  • Despite her behaviour, she was highly intelligent and well educated and had a degree from London University.
  • This report describes the coroner's inquest into her death in May 1913. At the Derby race she tried to grab the reins of the King's horse but was struck and killed in the attempt. Nobody really knows whether she was trying to commit suicide or was trying to stop the horse in a sensational protest.
