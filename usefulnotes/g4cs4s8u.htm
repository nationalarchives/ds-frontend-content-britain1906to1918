source 8
Extract from the Report of the Board of Agriculture, October 1918
(PRO ref: MAF 59/2)		  
  • The Board of Agriculture was in charge of food production, and clearly this was very important during the Great War. This report was written as the war was coming to an end. The whole report was very large, but this extract deals with the contribution of women to the effort to produce enough food.
  • Before the war, a large proportion of Britain's food was imported. However, once war broke out Britain needed to feed itself more effectively.
  • This was because Britain needed space in ships to bring in vital war supplies (eg metal, rubber, machinery etc) and also because German U-Boats tried to shut off food supplies by torpedoing ships.
  • A lot of land, such as public parks, was turned over to crops. Farmers were encouraged to produce as much as possible.
  • Women played a key part in this process. Around 16,000 women joined the Women's Land Army (see source 2 in this case study). They played an important role. However, a much bigger role was played by women who already lived in farming areas.
  • Women were involved in organising production and getting workers where they were needed. In many cases, women's suffrage supporters took a leading role in this, often working alongside men and women who had opposed women's suffrage before the war.
