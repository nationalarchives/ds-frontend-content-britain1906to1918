Extract from a confidential report for the British government on unemployment published in 1910. This section deals with the effects of underemployment
(PRO ref: CAB 37/96/159)		  
  • This document was put to the Cabinet in December 1908, soon before the Liberals introduced their own system of Labour Exchanges for unemployed workers.
  • The author of the report was Winston Churchill.
  • At the time, Britain was the richest country in Europe by some way. However, at the same time its poor were the poorest and unhealthiest in Europe.
  • Many of Britain's trades were reliant on casual workers who could be taken on or laid off for a day's work at a time, sometimes even a few hours work.
  • Unemployed workers who were laid off had to simply walk the streets asking at factories and similar places to see if there was work.
  • There were some labour exchanges in existence, which tried to tell workers about jobs. Many, however, were run by trade unions and gave priority to their own members. Very often the most vulnerable workers did not belong to unions.
