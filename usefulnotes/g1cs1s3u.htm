Part of a letter written in 1903 by Mr Ernest Campagnac, an official in the Blackburn Board of Education
(PRO ref: PC 8/584)		  
  • This letter was written to a Mr Morant, a colleague of Mr Campagnac.
  • Mr Campagnac worked for the Board of Education in Blackburn in the early 1900s.
  • At the time, Blackburn was a big industrial town. Most people there, many of them women, worked in the textile mills.
  • Dr Ramsay and Dr Greenwood were local doctors who carried out medical inspections for the Blackburn Board of Education.
