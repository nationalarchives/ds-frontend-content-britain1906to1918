Labour Party proposals for measures to help the unemployed, published in 1907
(PRO ref: PRO 30/69/1787. Material in the copyright of Ramsay MacDonald is reproduced by permission of the granddaughter of the late Malcolm MacDonald.)		  
  • The Labour Party was small in the early 1900s but it grew rapidly. It had the support of many trade unions and was represented by passionate and committed MPs like Keir Hardie and Will Thorne (see source 5).
  • James Ramsay MacDonald was another important figure in the Labour Party. He went on to be Britain's first Labour Prime Minister in 1924.
  • In this document, MacDonald set out the problems which unemployment caused to working people and also the measures which Labour wanted to see put in place to tackle the problems.
  • The main solution put forward by Labour was a new Act of Parliament to help the unemployed. This proposed law contained 14 sections (clauses) in total. This extract contains just a few.
