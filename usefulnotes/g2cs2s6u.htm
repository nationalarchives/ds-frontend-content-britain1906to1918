source 6
An exchange of letters between government officials and Monmouthshire Education Committee officials on providing spectacles for children 1908
(PRO ref: ED 125/11)		  
  • The government introduced medical inspections to schools in 1907 as part of the Education (Administrative Provisions) Act. All local authorities had to provide medical checks but they did not have to provide treatment until 1912.
  • Some local authorities took their new powers further than others before 1912. Big city councils like Liverpool brought in extensive medical checks and treatment. So did some councils in South Wales.
  • If councils wanted to provide treatment like spectacles they had to convince the government's Board of Education that it would help the children's education.
  • Sources 6a and 6b show a small selection of the letters which went back and forth between the council and the Board of Education.
  • Source 6a shows the first letter. The Board of Education then wrote back and asked the council to make sure that the spectacles were needed, that no charities could provide them and that the parents really could not afford them. Monmouthshire replied that this was the case and then source 6b was sent back to Monmouthshire.
