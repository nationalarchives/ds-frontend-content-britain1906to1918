Part of an article in The Morning Leader newspaper, 29 October 1906
(Reproduced by permission of the British Library) 		 
  • Emmeline Pethick Lawrence was a leading member of the WSPU and also joint editor of the journal Votes For Women.
  • In this article the Morning Leader calls her a suffragist. The WSPU were more often called the Suffragettes, but the press often used the term for supporters of the women's cause generally.
  • Mrs Pethinck Lawrence was arrested by police after a disturbance outside the House of Commons. Large numbers of both WSPU and NUWSS members tried to push their way into Parliament. As a result there were scuffles and clashes between police and women.
  • Holloway was the main women's prison in London.
  • Tactics like these were shocking to people at the time. Most people had a particular view of women (especially better off women) as being delicate and ladylike rather than forceful or having strong views.
