Letter from the Ministry of Food encouraging food economy, March 1918
(PRO ref: ED 138/59)		  
  • Rationing was introduced in January 1918. It seems to have solved the problem of price rises but rationing did not alter the fact that certain goods were in short supply.
  • This document was originally sent by the Ministry of Food to the Board of Education in March 1918.
  • In April 1918 the Board of Education then sent it out to all local education authorities. This was because under the Education (School Meals) Act it was the responsibility of the local authority to decide whether they provided school meals and what was in those school meals.
  • It should be stressed that hundreds of letters like this went out from the Ministry of Food during the war. At different times, they warned the authorities of goods that were in short supply. The most common shortages were wheat and sugar.
