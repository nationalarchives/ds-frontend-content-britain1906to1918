Cartoon published in 1913 commenting on relations between Suffragettes and police
(George Morrow, November 1913, ©Punch, Ltd., pu02575) 		 
  • Punch magazine was a journal which commented on important political issues of the day, but also included funny stories and cartoons as well.
  • Punch was generally opposed to female suffrage, although some cartoonists who drew for it were not opposed.
  • The majority of cartoons did not exactly criticise the suffrage movement, but more often made fun of the Suffragettes for being silly or misguided. Sometimes cartoons portrayed them as hideous witches.
  • This cartoon comes from 1913, when militant Suffragettes were very active because the government had dropped another Bill which would have given some women the vote.
  • They stepped up their campaigns of militant protest and attacks on property. This led to many arrests and clashes with police.
  • The year 1913 also saw hunger strikes and serious injuries to Suffragettes.
  • The tango was a dance which was becoming fashionable at the time in which dancers held each other more closely than traditional dances.
