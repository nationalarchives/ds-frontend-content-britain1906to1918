Letter from the Amalgamated Society of Engineers to the Chancellor of the Exchequer, September 1916
(PRO ref: T 172/123)		  
  • The Gateshead Branch of the Amalgamated Society of Engineers sent this letter to the Chancellor of the Exchequer in 1916.
  • There is no doubt that the cost of living went up dramatically during the Great War. Government reports suggest that the overall cost of living (including food, heating, clothes etc) rose by an estimated 74% between 1914 and 1918.
  • It is not easy to work out exactly how this affected people because wages also rose dramatically during this period. Many pensioners were able to find some work to go alongside their pension.
