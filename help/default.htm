Using access keys for navigation
  Use your keyboard to navigate the Britain 1906-18 exhibition.

<shift> + <1> goes to Gallery 1: Liberal Welfare Reforms 1906-11
 <shift> + <2> goes to Gallery 2: Achievements of Liberal Reforms
 <shift> + <3> goes to Gallery 3: Early 1900s women's suffrage
 <shift> + <4> goes to Gallery 4: Gaining Women's Suffrage
 <shift> + <5> goes to Gallery 5: Civilians & War 1914-18
 <shift> + <6> goes to Gallery 6: Propaganda 1914-18
 <shift> + <7> goes to Gallery 7: Review & Revision

 <shift> + <@> goes to Britain 1906-18 home page

Note: These access keys use the English (United Kingdom) keyboard layout and are not available on the Big Question sections or the advanced search page.

Learning Curve accounts
  This Learning Curve exhibition introduces a new way of working in some of our "Big Question" activities. You can create your own account online, work on one of our activities, save your work and then resume working at a later date. In order to do this, you need to sign up for a Learning Curve account. You can still use the exhibition activities without signing up, but your work will not be saved.

In this exhibition there are two activities that you can complete and save online: one in Gaining Women's Suffrage and one in Review & Revision. Those sections have a "Log In" button with instructions to guide you through the process of creating a Learning Curve account.

Site search
  Enter a keyword or keywords into the search to find web pages within the Britain 1906-18 exhibition on the subject of your choice. Note: This search only looks for web pages within the Britain 1906-18 exhibition, not the whole Learning Curve website. It only looks for web pages, not other file formats (such as rtf, pdf, films).

You can search for any word or phrase that appears on the web pages in Britain 1906-18. Consecutive words are treated as a phrase, eg national insurance will find pages that have those two words appearing together in that order. To search for several words in a document, separate the words with a comma, eg women, work, war will find pages with some or all of these words, with the best matches first.

To create a more precise search you can use Boolean operators (AND, OR, and NOT) and the proximity operator (NEAR).
  • AND - Use to search for both terms in the same page, eg women and vote finds pages with both the words 'women' and 'vote'.
  • OR - Use to search for either term in a page, eg suffragette or suffragist finds pages with the word 'suffragette' or the word 'suffragist'.
    AND NOT - Use to search for the first term without the second term, eg war and not first finds pages with the word 'war' but not 'first'.
  • NEAR - Use to search for both terms close together in the same page, eg liberal near labour finds pages with these two words near to each other.
  • Use quotation marks (") to indicate that an operator should be ignored in your query, eg "words or phrases" will match pages with the phrase 'words or phrases', not pages that match the Boolean expression.
Use wildcard operators to help you find pages containing words similar to a given word.
  • * - Use to search for words with the same prefix, eg act* finds pages with words such as 'activities', 'activity', etc.
  • ** - Use to search for words based on the same stem word, eg fly** finds pages with words such as 'flying', 'flew', etc.

Gaining Women's Suffrage Big Question - creating an online exhibition
  The aim:
The Suffragette Hall of Fame that you create will have 9 sources in it, split into 4 sections:
  • Section 1 (sources 1, 2 and 3): on the group you think made the biggest impact towards some women gaining the vote in 1918
  • Section 2 (sources 4 and 5): on the next most important group
  • Section 3 (sources 6 and 7): on the third most important group
  • Section 4 (sources 8 and 9): on evidence which shows that the efforts of each group sometimes combined to have an impact

You will create your exhibition in order from source 1 to source 9. Note: it will be easier to create an exhibition if you complete the research stage first.

Section details:

As you begin each section, you will be asked to answer some questions about that section in general. These answers will cover all of the sources within the section, and can be edited at any point. There are no questions at the beginning of section 4.

Source details:

First you must choose a source. There are two ways to do this. If you already know which source you would like, simply select it from the drop-down menu. If you do not know the exact source you want to use, you can search for it by keyword, then select it from the list of search results. If you choose to use one of the film clips in your exhibition, you will get a still image from the film, not the moving film itself. Once you have chosen your source, you can add a date, title, and caption.

You can edit any of the sources you've chosen at any time. Simply change the text in any of the boxes, or use the "Change Source" button to completely remove a source.Removing a source will permanently delete all the text you have typed into the caption boxes associated with that source.

You can see how your exhibition will look by pressing the "View Exhibition" button. This will reformat your work into an interactive presentation. While viewing your exhibition you can also print it in a printer-friendly format by pressing the "Print Exhibition" button.

You can work on your exhibition for as long as you like during a single session, but you will need to log in if you want to save your work and come back to it another time. Important! You may lose your work if you are not logged in and you do not use the exhibition for more than 20 minutes - the computer does this automatically. Remember to save your work by logging in if you leave the computer or if you want to come back to your work another day.

Logging In:

The "Log In" button will take you to a login page. If you already have an account you can log in by entering your e-mail address and password. If you are a new user you can create a new Learning Curve account by filling in the details requested. (Mandatory fields are indicated by an asterisk, other fields are optional).
