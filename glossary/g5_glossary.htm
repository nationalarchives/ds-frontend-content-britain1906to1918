This glossary is organised in alphabetical order. If you are looking for a word (e.g. Exchequer) it might be part of a phrase (e.g. Chancellor of the Exchequer).

Absenteeism Being absent (away) from work
Acquaint Make familiar
Apparatus Equipment
Apprehension Concern about something (being apprehensive about …)
Attest Promise to join the Army when called on (this was a halfway house between volunteering and conscription)
Canvass Term used to describe a recruiting campaign, particularly the one organised by Lord Derby in late 1915
Compulsion A common term used for conscription (look up conscription)
Conscientious Relating to the conscience (e.g. conscientious objectors were not prepared to kill because they believed it was wrong)
Conscription Compulsory military service (could also be compulsory service in certain industries e.g. munitions)
Contravention Going against (e.g. breaking a rule)
Conveyance Carrying
Cordial Friendly
Cultivation Growing crops
Curtailment Reduction or holding back
Diminution Reducing or losing
Disaffection Discontent
Disclosure Letting someone know
Divergence of opinion Different views
Elicit Obtain, get hold of
Entailed Involved
Famine prices High prices caused by extreme shortages
Incumbent upon us Necessary duty we have to carry out
Intoxicants Alcoholic drinks
Lucrative Well paid
Necessities of life Basic things like food, clothes, fuel etc
Notwithstanding Even with this factor
Obligations Responsibilities (e.g. looking after a family)
Personation Faking
Pilotage Steering ships into port
Precarious Dangerous
Privy Council Advisers to the King
Prohibit Ban
Prussia Most important region of Germany
Reserved occupations Jobs where workers were too valuable to the war effort to be allowed to join up. Some jobs (e.g. coal miners) were only reserved for certain periods of the war.
Sacredness Holiness
Semaphore System of messages using flags
Slacker Insulting term used to describe men who had not joined the Army - suggesting they were cowards
Starred jobs Jobs that were important to the war economy, so only a certain number of workers in these jobs were allowed to join up
Violation Breaking or destroying
Want of courage Fear, or lack of bravery
Wangle To achieve by deception
Wastage Losses
