This glossary is organised in alphabetical order. If you are looking for a word (e.g. contraction) it might be part of a phrase (e.g. passing contraction).

Fatal to character Turning a good person into a rascal
Friendly Society Organisations of working people - people paid money to the societies and received sick pay if they were ill or injured and could not work
Fruitless Pointless
Heredity The effect of the family environment on children (e.g. inherited genetic disorders or simply poor health from being part of a poor family)
Impaired Made worse
Indiscriminately Chosen at random
Journeyman Ordinary working man, usually employed by a master craftsman or firm
Laborious Hard work
Legend Notice
Liberal One of the main political parties in Britain in the early 1900s. The other main party was the Conservatives. The Labour Party was small but growing.
Mortality Death
Necessitious In need
Passing depression An industry suffering temporary hard times
Pauperism Being poor enough to need help from the local council, charities or Poor Law authorities
Permanent contraction An industry in complete decline
Physical development Proper growth for the age of the child
Rickets / rickety A disease caused by lack of vitamins which results in children being rickety (bow legged)
Slum Area of poor housing
Subsisting Barely managing to live
Summons Calling someone
Superannuation Payment like a pension to people who have retired
Superfluous Not needed
Tenement Very poor type of housing, usually made of single room units in blocks. Aim was to get as many people in as possible.
Untoward Not very good
Want of food Lack of food
Welfare reforms Government laws or measures which aim to improve people's health, living conditions, working conditions or provide other kinds of protection
