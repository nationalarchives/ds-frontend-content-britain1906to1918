1914 August Outbreak of war.

Massive government recruitment campaign brought in 500,000 recruits in one month.

Government passed Defence of the Realm Act (DORA). DORA gave the government sweeping powers to take over land, run newspapers, control industries like coal etc.

  December First attacks on British civilians as German warships shelled Scarborough.
1915 January First air raids by Zeppelin airships on East Anglia. Raids spread across the country and were a feature of wartime life.
  May Different political parties in Britain came together to form a National Government to run the country as effectively as possible during the war.
  July Britain suffered a munitions crisis - private companies were simply unable to produce the huge quantities of shells, bullets and weapons needed. David Lloyd George became Minister of Munitions, controlling production of munitions and setting up many government-run munitions factories.
  Autumn Disputes between trade unions and government over women workers. Unions were afraid that employers would sack men to employ lower paid women. Government promised to pay women the same as men and safeguard jobs of men.

Government became increasingly concerned about the numbers volunteering to join the army and by arguments about whether conscription would be acceptable.
1916 January First Military Service Bill introduced, which conscripted all single men aged 18-40 to the armed forces.
  May Second Military Service Bill extended conscription to married men.
  July Battle of the Somme saw huge losses. By this stage conscription had not had much effect, so the British soldiers in the Somme were the last of the volunteer recruits.
  August Film of 'The Battle of the Somme' released - millions went to watch it.
  November Battle of the Somme ended.

First examples of criticism of politicians and military in the way they were handling the war.

  December David Lloyd George replaced Asquith as Prime Minister.
1917 February

Germany intensified submarine warfare against merchant ships.

Women's Land Army formed.
  April Food supplies in Britain reaching crisis point.
  November Voluntary rationing scheme introduced in Britain by the government, but public failed to observe it.
1918 February - April Government introduced compulsory rationing scheme to London and South East, then the rest of the country.
  November War ended.
