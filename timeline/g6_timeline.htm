1914 Outbreak of war.

Propaganda Bureau set up at Wellington House - its main focus was on spreading pro-British propaganda in the USA.

Under DORA regulations, Irish Nationalist journals suppressed.

Publication of 'Why We Are At War - Great Britain's Case' (which went on to sell 50,000 copies).
1915 The Times, The Labour Leader and London Figaro all fined heavily for breaking censorship rules.

British Propaganda Bureau publications reach the 2.5 million mark.

Formation of British Topical Committee for War Films.
1916 Film of 'The Battle of the Somme' released - millions went to watch it. Approximately 9 million went to see the film 'For the Empire'.

Government officials studied 38,000 newspaper articles and 300,000 private telegrams.

Government took over the British Topical Committee for War Films and began to make its own.
1917 Ministry of Information set up under Sir Edward Carson but largely run by novelist John Buchan.

National War Aims Committee set up.

Release of the film 'The German Retreat and the Battle of Arras'.
1918 Ministry of Information set up under Lord Beaverbrook.
